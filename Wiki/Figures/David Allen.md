American productivity consultant and author of *Getting Things Done: The Art of Stress-Free Productivity* (2001). The book introduced the [[Getting Things Done]] (GTD) methodology, which became one of the most influential frameworks in [[Personal Knowledge Management]].

Allen's central thesis — that the mind is for having ideas, not holding them — shifted mainstream thinking about productivity from time management toward *attention* management. His work predates the digital PKM movement but laid the groundwork for it: the emphasis on trusted external systems, regular review, and inbox-zero thinking runs through virtually every modern knowledge management tool and methodology.
